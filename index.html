Banff National Park

Banff National Park is Canada's oldest national park and one of the most visited tourist attractions in North America. Located in the Rocky Mountains, it offers breathtaking views of glaciers, mountains, and lakes.

Things to Do

Hiking

There are over 1,600 kilometers of hiking trails in Banff National Park, ranging from easy walks to strenuous multi-day hikes. Some of the most popular hikes include Johnston Canyon, Lake Agnes, and Plain of Six Glaciers.

Camping

There are over 2,000 campsites in Banff National Park, with a variety of options ranging from primitive sites to RV sites with full hookups. Some of the most popular campgrounds include Tunnel Mountain, Two Jack Lakeside, and Lake Louise.

Skiing and Snowboarding

Banff National Park is home to three ski resorts: Banff Mount Norquay, The Lake Louise Ski Resort, and Sunshine Village. With over 8,000 acres of skiable terrain, there are options for skiers and snowboarders of all skill levels.
